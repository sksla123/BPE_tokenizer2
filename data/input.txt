ebookHere are some famous and inspiring English quotes:

"To be, or not to be, that is the question." This phrase, from William Shakespeare's Hamlet, reflects the human condition and the existential choices we face.

"The only thing we have to fear is fear itself." Franklin D. Roosevelt's words from his First Inaugural Address in 1933 remind us that fear can be our greatest obstacle.

"I think, therefore I am." René Descartes' philosophical statement from Discourse on the Method emphasizes the connection between thought and existence.

"The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams." Eleanor Roosevelt's words encourage us to pursue our aspirations with optimism and faith.

"Ask not what your country can do for you - ask what you can do for your country." John F. Kennedy's Inaugural Address in 1961 calls upon individuals to contribute to the greater good.

"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." Neil Armstrong's famous phrase during the Moon Landing in 1969 captures the essence of human achievement and progress.

"The pen is mightier than the sword." This phrase, coined by Edward Bulwer-Lytton in Richelieu; Or the Conspiracy, highlights the power of ideas and communication over violence.

"Life is what happens when you’re busy making other plans." John Lennon's words remind us that life often unfolds unexpectedly.

"You must be the change you wish to see in the world." Mahatma Gandhi's philosophy emphasizes personal responsibility and action in creating positive change.

"Believe you can and you're halfway there." Theodore Roosevelt's advice encourages us to have faith in our abilities.

"It does not matter how slowly you go as long as you do not stop." This ancient wisdom from Confucius' Analects emphasizes perseverance and consistency.

"Rome was not built in a day." This proverb reminds us that great achievements require time and effort.

"Time is gold." This saying underscores the value of time and the importance of using it wisely.

"Heaven helps those who help themselves." This phrase encourages self-reliance and proactive effort in achieving our goals.

"Every wall is a door." This metaphor suggests that obstacles can also be opportunities, depending on our perspective.
